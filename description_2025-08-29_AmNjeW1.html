Alright, listen up, buttercup. I'm gonna give you the lowdown on this Sufjan Stevens fella's albums like only Kid Rock can. This is gonna be straight-shootin', no-nonsense, red, white, and blue honesty, ya hear? • A Sun Came - 2/10. What in the yee-haw is this psychedelic, folksy nonsense? Sounds like a bunch of hippies jammin' in a yurt. Ain't got no heartland rockin' to it. • Enjoy Your Rabbit - 0/10. Electronic noise? Animal sounds? This ain't music, it's a dang headache. If I wanna hear rabbits, I'll go huntin'. • Michigan - 6/10. Alright, this one's got some grit. He's singin' about my stompin' grounds. It's a little too soft around the edges, but I can respect the subject matter. • Seven Swans - 3/10. More of that whispery, folky stuff. Sounds like church music for folks who don't like church. Pass. • Illinois - 7/10. Okay, now we're talkin'. A whole album about Illinois? That's kinda badass. Still a little too artsy-fartsy for my taste, but the ambition's there. He's wearin' that blue and yellow, brother. • The Age of Adz - 1/10. What in the holy hell is this garbage? Sounds like a robot having a bad trip. Too weird, even for me. • Carrie & Lowell - 4/10. Depressing acoustic ballads? I come to music to party, not to cry in my beer. This is a god dang depressing listen. • Planetarium - 0/10. Stars? Planets? Give me a cold one and a bonfire. This is like space music for nerds. • Aporia - 1/10. More electronic bloop and bleep. I would not listen to this for fun. • The Ascension - 2/10. This one's as boring as watching paint dry. I do not care about this album. I can't even listen to it. • A Beginner’s Mind - 2/10. He's got a point about movies. However, I would rather not listen to this album ever again. • Javelin - 3/10. All I can say about this is that he does try to make this music enjoyable, but I just can't get myself to. There you have it, buddy. Kid Rock's honest opinion on Sufjan Stevens.
