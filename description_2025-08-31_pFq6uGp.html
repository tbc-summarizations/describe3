Alright, alright, let's break down these Portishead albums, BEP style! • Dummy: 9/10. This album is FIRE! That moody trip-hop sound is just infectious. "Sour Times" and "Glory Box" are straight classics. Fergie would kill those vocals. • Portishead: 7/10. It's good, but doesn't quite hit the heights of Dummy. Still some cool grooves and haunting vibes, but a little more experimental in ways that don't always connect. • Roseland NYC Live: 6/10. Live albums are tough, especially when you're trying to capture that studio magic. It's solid, but the energy is a little flat for my taste. Where's the hype, people?! • Third: 3/10. Nah, this album is weird and boring. It's like they tried to be too artsy and forgot to bring the beats. Will.i.am wouldn't touch this with a ten-foot pole.
