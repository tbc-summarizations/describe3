Well, folks, let's be blunt about this Eastern European Cup final. Croatia barely squeaked by Hungary with a 1-0 victory, and honestly, it was about as thrilling as watching paint dry. First off, let's talk about Croatia. They had more possession, more shots, and a higher expected goals, but they only managed one measly goal. That's pathetic. Andrej Kramarić gets the goal and man of the match award, but let's be real, his performance was nothing special. He was simply the least mediocre player on the field. As for Luka Modrić, don't let the assist fool you, he's past his prime and was largely invisible for much of the match. The entire Croatian squad needs a serious wake-up call if they think they can compete at a higher level. Hungary, on the other hand, were completely toothless in attack. Their expected goals were an abysmal 0.7xG, which is just embarrassing. They had no creativity, no urgency, and looked like they were just happy to be there. Their midfield was non-existent, and their strikers couldn't hit the broad side of a barn. Honestly, they should be ashamed of their performance. The only positive thing I can say about this match is that it's finally over. It was a slog to watch, and neither team deserved to win. Eastern European football needs to seriously up its game if it wants to be taken seriously on the international stage. This was a disappointing final, and I hope to never see a match this boring again. And for the cherry on top. What on Earth is that image of an insect playing a violin in front of an ear?
