Alright, picture this: I'm spinning these records back-to-back at a groovy pad in '60s London. Here's the lowdown, straight up: • Björk - Vespertine: What in the name of psychedelic tea parties is this?! Sounds like a robot opera gone wrong. No way, man. This is not the happening sound. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: Okay, this cat Bowie... he's got something. A bit out there, but the tunes are catchy and the whole space alien thing is kinda cool. Still, a bit too theatrical for my taste. • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: This is just noise, plain and simple. No melody, no groove, just a bunch of instruments noodling around for way too long. Get this off my turntable! • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Oh dear, this is just depressing! • Jeff Buckley - Grace: This Buckley fellow has some serious pipes. Reminds me of a young Sinatra, but with a more rockin' edge. A bit overwrought, but I dig it. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: What is this racket? Sounds like a bunch of angry poets shouting over some funky music. Nope, this is no good to me. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: Now this is more like it! A bit weird, but in a good way. The Mellotron sounds like something from outer space, and the songs are actually interesting. I can dig it. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: This is like the soundtrack to a bad dream, with a feminist twist. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: More noise. This sounds like a bunch of guys yelling about stuff. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Marvin Gaye is a genius. This album is a masterpiece of soul music, with a message that's more important than ever. A must-have for any serious record collection. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Now this is some serious jazz. Mingus is a madman, but he's a genius. This album is like a wild, chaotic dream, full of passion and fire. • Nas - Illmatic: This Nas fella is just yelling about stuff. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: This is Pink Floyd? After Syd Barrett left? This sucks. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: Okay, this Prince character is... interesting. A bit too flashy for my taste, but he's got some serious talent. The songs are catchy, the guitar playing is killer, and the whole thing is just plain fun. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: More noise. No thank you. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Stevie Wonder is a true original. This album is a celebration of life, love, and music. It's got everything from soulful ballads to funky jams. A must-have for any serious record collection. • System of a Down - Toxicity: More noise, can't deal. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: The Beatles? What can I say? They're the greatest band of all time. This album is a perfect example of their genius, with its catchy melodies, innovative arrangements, and timeless lyrics. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: More noise again, I don't like this. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): What in the world? Sounds like a bunch of gangsters shouting over some scratchy records. Nope, this ain't my bag at all.
