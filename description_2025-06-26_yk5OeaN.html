Alright, here's my brutally honest assessment of those Belgian clubs, ranked out of 10 based on their overall significance, history, and current standing within Belgian football: • Anderlecht: 9/10. This is the top dog. Historically the most dominant club in Belgium, a ton of titles, consistent European presence, and a massive fanbase. They might have dips in form, but their overall impact and legacy are undeniable. • Beerschot: 3/10. A smaller club from Antwerp, plagued by financial issues and inconsistency. They have some history, but they're not a major force. They've been in and out of the top league too much. • Cercle Brugge: 4/10. Always in the shadow of their bigger brothers. Not a particularly strong team. • Club Brugge: 8.5/10. Always trying to compete with Anderlecht and consistently good. • Dender: 2/10. A smaller team, has never been relevant. • KAA Gent: 7.5/10. Good history, has recently been very good. • KRC Genk: 7.5/10. Solid club with a good track record of developing talent and has won the league a few times. • KV Kortrijk: 3/10. Smaller club that is inconsistent. • KV Mechelen: 6/10. Used to be the best team in Belgium but has declined. • Oud-Heverlee Leuven: 4/10. Only been in the top flight for 20 years. • Royal Antwerp: 7/10. Oldest club in the country, great fan base. • Sporting Charleroi: 5/10. Not that big of a club. • Standard Liege: 7/10. One of the biggest clubs in the country. • STVV: 4/10. Nothing special. • Union Saint-Gilloise: 7/10. A club that is rapidly rising and has been really good recently. • Westerlo: 3/10. Always a team that is around and has some decent play.
