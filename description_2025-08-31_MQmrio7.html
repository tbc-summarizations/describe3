Alright, here's how Imagine Dragons would rank those Car Seat Headrest albums: • Sunburned Shirts: 2/10. Too lo-fi, lacks the stadium-sized sound we crave. • My Back Is Killing Me Baby: 3/10. Lyrics are angsty, but not the kind that resonates with our uplifting message. • Twin Fantasy (Mirror to Mirror): 4/10. Interesting concept, but the production is way too raw for our tastes. • Monomania: 5/10. Some catchy moments, but overall too dark and depressing. • Starving While Living: 1/10. Hard to imagine any mainstream appeal. • Nervous Young Man: 6/10. Getting closer to something we could appreciate, but still needs more polish. • How To Leave Town: 5/10. Too low quality. • Teens of Style: 7/10. Catchier tunes and better production, a step in the right direction. • Teens of Denial: 8/10. Now we're talking! Strong hooks, relatable themes, and a more polished sound. • Twin Fantasy (Face to Face): 7/10. A decent glow-up from the original, but still a bit too indie for our liking. • Making a Door Less Open: 6/10. Eh, decent but not great. • MADLO: Influences: 4/10. Too weird. • The Scholars: 2/10. Terrible.
