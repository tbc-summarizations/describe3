Alright, here's my brutally honest take on those tracks: • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Sleep: Overhyped, pretentious drone. It's the kind of thing hipsters listen to feel sophisticated. 4/10 • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Static: See above. Just more of the same self-indulgent garbage. 3/10 • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Storm: Still riding the same tired formula. How many minutes of drawn-out nothingness do we need? 2/10 • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Like Antennas to Heaven...: Okay, MAYBE this one has a little something going on. But the payoff takes way too long. 5/10 • Mingus - Track D: Now we're talking! Raw, chaotic, and brilliant. This is real music. 9/10 • John Coltrane - Part III - Pursuance / IV - Psalm: Spiritual jazz at its finest. Coltrane was a genius. 10/10 • Joanna Newsom - Only Skin: That voice is an acquired taste, but the song is genuinely beautiful and weird. 7/10 • The Jimi Hendrix Experience - Voodoo Chile: One of the greatest guitar songs ever written, hands down. 10/10 • The Jimi Hendrix Experience - 1983... (A Merman I Should Turn to Be): Ambitious and psychedelic. A bit too long, but still incredible. 8/10 • Pink Floyd - Shine On You Crazy Diamond (1-5): A classic for a reason. Atmospheric, epic, and iconic. 9/10 • Kanye West - Last Call: Funny, arrogant, and a great storytelling track. Kanye at his peak (musically). 8/10 • Black Country, New Road - Basketball Shoes: Overrated. A mess of noise and emotion that doesn't quite gel. 5/10 • Pink Floyd - Shine On You Crazy Diamond (6-9): The second half isn't quite as good as the first, but still essential. 8/10 • King Crimson - Moonchild: Ethereal and haunting. A perfect interlude on a classic album. 7/10 • Joanna Newsom - Emily: Another Newsom track with a compelling melody. Not as good as Only Skin. 6/10 • Kendrick Lamar - Mortal Man: A powerful and thought-provoking song. Kendrick's lyrical ability is unmatched. 9/10 • Miles Davis - All Blues: Relaxing and cool as hell. A jazz standard for a reason. 10/10 • Have a Nice Life - Earthmover: Depressing and emotionally draining. It's a powerful experience but I wouldn't listen to this often. 7/10 • Daft Punk - Too Long: A masterpiece of electronic music. Repetitive in a good way. 10/10
