Alright, here's my brutally honest take on these transfers, rated out of 10, with no sugarcoating: Florian Wirtz (Bayer Leverkusen to Liverpool): 10/10. This is a game-changer. Wirtz is a generational talent, and Liverpool is getting him at what is potentially the perfect time. He would give Klopp a world-class creative midfielder, he's got the potential to become a club legend at Anfield. This is a statement signing. Matheus Cunha (Wolves to Manchester United): 4/10. What are they thinking? Cunha has flashes of brilliance, but is inconsistent and lacks the killer instinct United desperately needs. He's not the answer to their goalscoring problems, and this feels like a panic buy. Erik Ten Hag would have been better off developing a young player. Martin Zubimendi (Real Sociedad to Arsenal): 7/10. A solid, if unspectacular, addition. Zubimendi is a reliable defensive midfielder who will provide stability to Arsenal's midfield. He's not a world-beater, but he's a significant upgrade over what they currently have in that position. Arteta needs a better holding midfielder and Zubimendi fits the system. Jamie Gittens (Borussia Dortmund to Chelsea): 2/10. Another "project" player for Chelsea? Gittens has shown flashes of talent, but he's far from the finished product. Chelsea already has a squad full of unproven youngsters, and this just adds to the chaos. He'll spend half of his time injured and the other half rotting on the bench. A waste of money. Mohammed Kudus (West Ham to Tottenham): 8/10. Excellent business for Spurs. Kudus is a dynamic, versatile attacker who will thrive under Postecoglou's attacking style.
