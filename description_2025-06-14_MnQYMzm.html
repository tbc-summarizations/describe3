Alright, here's my brutally honest assessment of France's 1998 World Cup-winning starting XI, both individually and as a team: Individual Player Ratings (Out of 10): • Fabien Barthez: 7 - Eccentric and prone to the occasional howler, but undeniably a good shot-stopper and a character. Not world-class, but dependable for France. • Lilian Thuram: 9 - Phenomenal defender. Strong, intelligent, and surprisingly capable going forward. Scored both goals in the semi-final against Croatia, which elevates him even higher. • Frank Leboeuf: 6 - Solid but unspectacular. Always a reliable presence, but definitely the weak link in the central defense pairing. A journeyman compared to Desailly. • Marcel Desailly: 9 - A rock. Strong, commanding, and fantastic in the air. A world-class defender who anchored the defense with authority. A true leader. • Bixente Lizarazu: 7.5 - A very good left-back. Energetic, intelligent defensively, and contributed to the attack. Consistent but not an all-time great. • Didier Deschamps: 8 - The water carrier. Not the flashiest player, but he did his job of protecting the back four and setting the tempo. A true captain and leader, even if his passing was at times basic. • Christian Karembeu: 5 - Surprising starter in the final. Hardworking, but limited technically. Often a liability in possession. Very strange selection. • Emmanuel Petit: 8.5 - A box-to-box midfielder with a great engine and a knack for scoring important goals. His goal in the final sealed the victory. Underrated. • Zinedine Zidane: 9.5 - The talisman. A genius with the ball at his feet. His two goals in the final were deserved. No player could control the ball as good as him. • Youri Djorkaeff: 7 - A decent player with good technical ability, but inconsistent. Tends to blow hot and cold. Never a truly top player. • Stéphane Guivarc'h: 3 - A donkey. Simply not good enough to be leading the line for a team of this caliber. No goals in the entire tournament and a passenger throughout. Team Rating (Out of 10): • Overall Team: 9.5 - This was a team built on a solid defensive base, with world-class talent in midfield, and the genius of Zidane to provide the spark. They lacked a truly top striker, but their defensive solidity and midfield dominance more than made up for it. They may have been a little boring and negative at times, but they got the job done. Undeniably worthy World Cup winners.
