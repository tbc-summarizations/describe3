Alright, let's tear this Inter Milan lineup apart with some cold, hard ratings: Individual Ratings: • Josep Martínez (GK): 6/10. He is a decent shot-stopper, but still lacks the experience and command to be a truly top-tier keeper. He is a bit of a gamble in a team with Scudetto aspirations. • Federico Dimarco (LB): 7.5/10. A workhorse with a great engine and decent technical ability. He's not the most defensively sound, but he is a threat going forward and has a good cross. • Alessandro Bastoni (CB): 8/10. Inter's best defender. He's composed, reads the game well, and is comfortable playing out from the back. A really important player. • Yann Bisseck (CB): 6/10. He is still unproven, and might be a liability against top-quality opposition. • Denzel Dumfries (RB): 6.5/10. His defending is often questionable and I don't think he is a great passer of the ball • Nicolò Barella (CM): 8/10. An absolute powerhouse in midfield. He is tireless, aggressive, and has a knack for arriving late in the box. A real driving force for the team. • Davide Frattesi (CM): 6.5/10. He is okay but unspectacular. • Hakan Çalhanoğlu (CM): 7.5/10. A solid deep-lying playmaker with great technique and a good range of passing. He lacks pace and mobility, but makes up for it with his vision. • Lautaro Martínez (ST): 9/10. The main man. He is a clinical finisher, has great movement, and leads the line brilliantly. One of the best strikers in Serie A. • Marcus Thuram (ST): 7/10. He is a good foil for Lautaro, but lacks the consistent goalscoring threat to be a true top striker. • Mehdi Taremi (ST): 6.5/10. Useful squad player, but ultimately a downgrade on what they had last season. Overall Team Rating: 7.5/10 This team is pretty good but relies heavily on a few key players like Bastoni and Lautaro, and the overall quality of the team has slightly gone down. This lineup is good enough to compete for a Champions League spot, but I just don't see them going all the way in the competition or winning the league. They lack a certain cutting edge and depth in key positions, and some of the players simply aren't good enough to play at the highest level.
