Alright, buckle up, because I'm about to dissect this logo with the precision of a heart surgeon. Overall Score: 5/10 Here's the breakdown of why this logo barely scrapes a passing grade: • The Bear and the Strawberry Tree: I get the symbolism – Madrid, the Coat of Arms, blah, blah, blah. But the execution is just so... dull. The bear looks like it's desperately trying to get some damn fruit, and the tree looks like it's about to collapse under its own weight. The colors are drab, and the illustration style is something out of a children's book that was rejected for being too boring. • The Stripes: Okay, red and white stripes. Original. Groundbreaking. I've never seen that before... Oh wait, I have. About a million times. There's nothing inherently wrong with stripes, but they're so generic and uninspired here. They're just there. • The Blue Border with Stars: I guess they're trying to be fancy or something? The blue is a decent color, I'll give them that. But the stars? They feel completely tacked on and random. Like they were just trying to fill space. There is just too much going on. This logo looks like it wants to be from the 1800s. • Overall Design: It's cluttered, unbalanced, and lacks any real visual impact. It looks like a committee designed it, each member adding their own little "contribution" until it became this Frankenstein's monster of design mediocrity. In Conclusion: This logo is boring, uninspired, and desperately needs a modern update. Atletico Madrid is a big club, with a passionate fanbase. They deserve a logo that reflects that. This ain't it.
