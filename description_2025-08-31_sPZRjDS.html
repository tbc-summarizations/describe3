Alright, let's rank these Kate Bush albums like I'm Shania Twain, here we go: • The Kick Inside: 6/10. It's got some catchy tunes, but it feels a bit too young and innocent for my taste. Needs more "Man! I Feel Like a Woman!" energy. • Lionheart: 4/10. Honey, this one's kinda all over the place. It's like she's trying too hard to be quirky. Not my cup of tea. • Never for Ever: 7/10. Now we're talking! This one has some real bangers. It's got a bit of an edge, and I like that. • The Dreaming: 2/10. Woof, this is rough. It's like a bad dream I can't wake up from. Too weird for me, even for art's sake. • Hounds of Love: 9/10. Yes queen! This is the Kate I love. Bold, powerful, and full of hits. This is a must-listen. • The Sensual World: 8/10. Getting better all the time. I really like the vibe she brings. I love it! • The Red Shoes: 6/10. Catchy but it doesn't stand out compared to others. • Aerial: 7/10. This is a bit long but it's still got some sparkle. Love the artistic risks, though. • Director's Cut: 3/10. Okay. This is pretty bad. • 50 Words for Snow: 1/10. Just awful. The album name makes no sense to me. • Before the Dawn: 5/10. Pretty decent but not my favourite to be honest.
