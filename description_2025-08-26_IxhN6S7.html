Alright, here's my brutally honest take as a pop music fan on those albums: • Björk - Vespertine: A bit too weird and experimental for my taste. Some of the sounds are interesting, but it's not something I'd listen to for fun. 4/10 • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: I can appreciate the theatrics and the concept, and a couple of the songs are catchy, but it's not a consistent album for me. 6/10 • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: Absolutely not. This is just droning noise. No pop sensibilities whatsoever. 1/10 • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Depressing and bleak. It's not pop and it's not enjoyable. 2/10 • Jeff Buckley - Grace: His voice is amazing, but the songs are a bit too serious and melancholic for my pop-loving heart. 5/10 • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: I can see why people like it, and there are some cool beats, but it's too preachy for me. 6/10 • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: This is just strange and dated. Not something I would ever listen to again. 3/10 • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: Now we're talking! This is a classic, and it has some great pop-infused R&B tracks. 9/10 • Madvillain - Madvillainy: The rapping is okay, but the beats are too lo-fi and weird for my taste. 5/10 • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: A soulful album with a message, but it's not exactly upbeat pop. 6/10 • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Absolute free-form jazz chaos. I have no idea what's going on. 1/10 • Nas - Illmatic: A classic hip-hop album. The lyrics are great, but it's not exactly "pop." 6/10 • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Too long, drawn-out, and boring. Give me something with a beat! 4/10 • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: YES! This is a pop masterpiece. Catchy songs, great vocals, and amazing guitar. 10/10 • Radiohead - In Rainbows: Depressing and too experimental. 5/10 • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: A fantastic album. Full of catchy songs, great melodies, and amazing musicianship. 9/10 • System of a Down - Toxicity: Some interesting riffs, but the vocals are way too aggressive for my taste. 4/10 • The Beatles - Abbey Road: A classic for a reason. Lots of catchy songs and innovative production. 8/10 • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: This is just noise. A low fidelity mess. 2/10 • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): Some cool beats and rhymes, but it's not my style. 6/10
