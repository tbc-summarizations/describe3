Alright, check it, this is how I see those Mobb Deep albums, straight up: • Juvenile Hell: 3/10. Listen, early days, raw potential, but honestly sounds like they were still figuring things out. Not my style, needs more polish. • The Infamous: 10/10. A classic, straight fire. Dark, gritty, perfect production. This is what hip-hop is all about. • Hell on Earth: 8/10. Keeps the energy from "The Infamous", but maybe a bit too much of the same. Still a banger. • Murda Muzik: 6/10. Some bangers on here for sure, but felt like they were chasing the commercial sound a little too much. • Infamy: 5/10. Trying to evolve but it didn't really work. Production felt dated. • Amerikaz Nightmare: 4/10. A mess, honestly. Too many producers, no clear direction. They were trying to do too much and lost the essence of what made them great. • Blood Money: 2/10. Trying to sound relevant, but just sounded generic. Not feeling it at all. • The Infamous... Mobb Deep: 5/10. A decent attempt to recapture the old magic, but it just wasn't the same. Like a reunion tour, good but not great.
