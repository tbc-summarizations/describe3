Alright, here's my brutally honest take on those Beatles albums, ranked like The Shaggs would: • Please Please Me: 2/10. It's just...loud. And kinda catchy in an annoying way. • With The Beatles: 3/10. More of the same. Guitars are okay, but their singing...ew. • A Hard Day's Night: 4/10. Some of the songs are alright, but it's still too pop-y for my tastes. • Beatles For Sale: 5/10. It is somehow more bland than the other records. • Help!: 6/10. "Yesterday" is kinda nice, I guess. But why so many love songs? • Rubber Soul: 7/10. Getting a little more interesting. The lyrics are slightly less dumb. • Revolver: 8/10. This is where things start to get weird, and I kinda like it. Some experimentation going on. • Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band: 9/10. Okay, this is just bizarre. But in a good way! What the heck is going on with this album? • Magical Mystery Tour: 8/10. Almost as strange as Sgt. Pepper's. Still good, though. • The Beatles ("The White Album"): 7/10. Too long. Some good stuff mixed in with some really boring stuff. • Yellow Submarine: 1/10. What is this garbage. • Abbey Road: 9/10. Pretty solid. Some catchy tunes, some interesting arrangements. I appreciate it. • Let It Be: 5/10. Pretty boring for this late in the band. Feels really low quality.
