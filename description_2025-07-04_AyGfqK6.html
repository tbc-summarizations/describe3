Alright, here's my take on legends for each of those clubs: • Atyrau: Not going to lie, this team isn't exactly overflowing with global icons. Based on what info I can dig up, I'll go with Konstantin Zadorozhny. He seems to be one of their more consistent goalscorers in recent history. • Atlético Madrid: Luis Aragonés. No contest. He embodies the spirit of the club both as a player and a manager. • Manchester City: Sergio Agüero. Their all-time leading scorer and a pivotal figure in their transformation into a dominant force. • AC Milan: Paolo Maldini. An absolute rock at the back, embodying the club's tradition of defensive excellence and longevity. • Beşiktaş: Süleyman Seba. More of an administrative legend. He was the president who shaped the modern era of Beşiktaş, and no player comes close to his impact. • Bayern Munich: Franz Beckenbauer. Der Kaiser. Elegant, dominant, and a true icon of German football. • Qarabağ: Rashad Sadygov. A long-serving captain and defender who led them to multiple league titles and European appearances. • Shakhtar Donetsk: Darijo Srna. A modern icon, the captain who led them through their most successful period, even during incredibly difficult circumstances. • Zenit St. Petersburg: Andrey Arshavin. Yeah, his time at Arsenal wasn't consistent, but he was electric for Zenit and a key figure in their UEFA Cup win. • Rangers: Ally McCoist. Top goal scorer and has a good history with club.
