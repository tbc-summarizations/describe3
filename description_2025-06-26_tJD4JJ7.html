Alright, let's break down this physics problem. Here's how to solve it: Understanding the Problem • MRUV: The bus is undergoing Uniformly Accelerated Rectilinear Motion (MRUV). This means it has constant acceleration. • Initial Conditions: The bus starts from rest (v = 0). • First Phase: The bus enters the tunnel completely in 2 seconds. During this time, the front of the bus travels a distance equal to the length of the tunnel (L). • Second Phase: We need to find the additional time it takes for the bus to completely exit the tunnel. This means the back of the bus needs to travel the length of the tunnel (L) plus the length of the bus (6m) in addition to whatever distance the front of the bus travels. • Final Velocity: The bus has a velocity of 10 m/s when it completely exits the tunnel. Relate the Total Velocity (10 m/s): • The final velocity of the front of the bus when it completely exits the tunnel is 10 m/s.
