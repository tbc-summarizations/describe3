Alright, let's dive into this batch of records as if it's the prime of the 1980s. Forget what comes later; we're judging these albums based on what's hot and what's not right now. No looking into the future! Björk - Vespertine: (3/10) Honestly, what is this? It sounds like something you'd hear in an art gallery – cold, distant, and completely lacking in anything resembling a catchy hook. This weird electronic stuff might appeal to some, but it's pretentious garbage. Good luck dancing to this. David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: (9/10) Okay, this is more like it! Bowie is a total space-age rock god. Ziggy Stardust is weird, but in a cool, theatrical way. Great guitar riffs, killer vocals, and the songs stick in your head. This album is pretty important and pretty cool. Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: (1/10) Are you serious? Four sides of instrumental garbage that goes nowhere. Sounds like a bunch of noise, or something for hippies and nerds. Turn this off. Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: (1/10) I can barely understand what's being said. It's depressing, and if this is the future of music, I'd rather listen to dead air. Don't have a nice life if this is playing. Jeff Buckley - Grace: (6/10) This guy has a hell of a voice, but the whole thing is just a little too melodramatic and sad. Some of the guitar work is pretty impressive, but the songs themselves lack the punch that real rock music needs. Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: (2/10) So much hype and what's delivered is so little. There are some clever lyrics but overall, it's one long song that goes on and on. King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: (7/10) This album is weird, atmospheric, and honestly pretty cool. The opening track is a classic, and the rest of the album has a dark, mystical feel. Definitely not for everyone, but you can get into it. Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: (8/10) I respect Lauryn Hill and her talents but this isn't for me. There are a lot of good songs here, like Doo Wop, and overall she is talented. Madvillain - Madvillainy: (6/10) I do have to say that these are some creative flows here. However, I don't know what the hell they are doing at some points. Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: (10/10) This is an all-time great. Soulful, powerful, and the songs are timeless. The message is important, and the music is smooth as silk. Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: (4/10) Avant-garde jazz? I am not for it. This is just the same thing going on for an hour. Nas - Illmatic: (7/10) This is a solid hip-hop record with some serious skills on display. The beats are hard, the lyrics are sharp, and the overall vibe is tough. It's a good snapshot of street life, but not my favorite thing to listen to. Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: (8/10) Pink Floyd continues to deliver a beautiful album with the song "Wish You Were Here". It's the only song from Pink Floyd I like. Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: (10/10) This album is everything. Prince is a genius – a total rock star with amazing songs, killer guitar skills, and pure charisma. "Purple Rain" is a masterpiece. Radiohead - In Rainbows: (4/10) Depressing, and boring, and it just drags on. Can't wait for it to be over. Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: (10/10) Stevie Wonder is a musical genius, and this album is proof. From soulful ballads to funky jams, it's got something for everyone. A feel-good masterpiece. System of a Down - Toxicity: (2/10) The singer is screaming at me, and they are just screaming about random things that don't make any sense. The Beatles - Abbey Road: (9/10) Even though it's old, Abbey Road is still a classic. The Beatles know what they're doing, and it shows. Some of their best work. The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: (5/10) There are a lot of instruments here, but nothing that I enjoy. It's kind of all over the place. Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): (8/10) This is hardcore hip-hop. Raw, gritty, and with a unique style. The beats are simple but effective, and the rapping is intense. It's not for the faint of heart, but it's a definite game-changer.
