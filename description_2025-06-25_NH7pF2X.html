2015-16 Astana Champions League Campaign Rating: 3/10 Here's the brutal truth: Astana's Champions League campaign was a lukewarm at best. They scrapped through qualifying against decidedly mediocre opposition, and barely managed to snag a single win. They drew far too many games in the group stage (four out of six) and that's just pathetic. This is supposed to be the CL, not some amateur league where draws are celebrated. It's about winning, dominating, and asserting your superiority. Let's break it down: • Qualifying: Beating Maribor and HJK Helsinki is nothing to brag about. These are not Champions League powerhouses, they barely managed to squeeze by, suggesting a lack of real quality in the team. It was ugly, unconvincing, and reeked of luck rather than tactical brilliance. • Group Stage: A pathetic showing. Zero wins against Benfica, Atletico, and Galatasaray is a disgrace. Those draws against Galatasaray and Benfica were lucky flukes and nothing more. Conceding 11 goals in 6 games is just a joke, their defense was non existent. • Overall: This was a forgettable campaign. No wins in the group stage, scraping through qualifying. It was embarrassing that a team like Astana was even allowed to compete at this stage of the competition. They contributed nothing of value to the Champions League, aside from being an easy opponent for others to get points from.
