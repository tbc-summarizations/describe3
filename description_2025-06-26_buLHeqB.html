Alright, here's the brutally honest rating of those Italian clubs, in my opinion: • Atalanta: 7/10. Consistently punching above their weight. Their recent run under Gasperini has been impressive. They play exciting football. • Bologna: 5/10. Can be entertaining at times, but far from consistent. Never really challenge for anything significant. • Cagliari: 4/10. Struggle to stay in Serie A. Often a yo-yo club. • Como: 3/10. They are in Serie B and have a long way to prove themselves. • Empoli: 5/10. Often play attractive football for a smaller team, but their results are inconsistent. A selling club. • Fiorentina: 6/10. Potential for greatness but often underachieve. • Genoa: 4/10. Historic club but often in turmoil. Struggle to compete in Serie A. • Hellas Verona: 5/10. Decent for a small team. • Inter Milan: 9/10. One of the giants. Always competing for the title. History, great players, and financial power. • Juventus: 9/10. Despite recent issues, they are the most dominant team in Italian football history. Still a force to be reckoned with. • Lazio: 7/10. Can challenge for European spots but are inconsistent. • Lecce: 4/10. Often in Serie B and are in turmoil and have little to no budget. • Milan: 9/10. One of the most successful clubs in Europe. Always in the hunt for major trophies. • Monza: 5/10. Can be entertaining. • Napoli: 8/10. Scudetto last season will be hard to repeat, but a really likeable and dangerous team. • Parma: 5/10. Have a good fan base but struggle to be relevant. • Roma: 8/10. Big club with a passionate fanbase. Underachieve a lot. • Torino: 5/10. Mid-table mediocrity is their usual level. Not much excitement. • Udinese: 5/10. Known for developing young talent, but never truly competitive. • Venezia: 3/10. Struggle to stay in Serie A. Often a yo-yo club.
