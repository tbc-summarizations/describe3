Alright, here's my brutally honest take on each of these American clubs and their greatness in the country, ranked out of 10: • Atlanta United: 9/10. They came in hot, set attendance records, won a title quickly, and built a strong brand. They've cooled off a bit, but the impact they had on MLS is undeniable. • Austin FC: 6/10. Good vibes and a cool stadium, but let's be real, they haven't achieved much yet. More hype than substance currently. • CF Montreal: 4/10. They're Canadian, but I'll include them. Constantly overshadowed, ownership issues, and a revolving door of players. Not a powerhouse. • Charlotte FC: 3/10. Expansion team struggles are expected, but they haven't shown anything particularly inspiring yet. • Chicago Fire FC: 5/10. Historically important, but massively underachieving for a major market. They need a serious reboot. • Colorado Rapids: 4/10. Always seem to be quietly existing. Rarely exciting, rarely terrible. Just... there. • Columbus Crew: 7/10. They have history and a dedicated fanbase, especially after nearly moving. Decent team, but not elite. • D.C. United: 6/10. Used to be a dynasty, now they're a shadow of their former selves. Needs a complete overhaul to get back to relevance. • FC Cincinnati: 7/10. Went from laughing stock to a legitimately good team fast. They have potential to keep climbing. • FC Dallas: 6/10. Consistently develops good young players, but rarely makes a deep playoff run. • Houston Dynamo: 5/10. Occasionally flashes of brilliance, but mostly mediocre. Not a team that strikes fear into opponents. • Inter Miami: 4/10. Overhyped because of Beckham, but haven't achieved much on the field. They need to start winning. • LA Galaxy: 8/10. A historical giant with a ton of MLS Cups, but they've been coasting on that reputation for too long. • Los Angeles FC: 9/10. Dominant on the field, a great stadium, and a real rivalry with the Galaxy. They've become a force in MLS. • Minnesota United FC: 5/10. A decent team with a cool stadium, but haven't reached the top tier yet. • Nashville SC: 7/10. Solid defensively and have made a splash in the playoffs a few times. A tough team to play against. • New England Revolution: 6/10. Often overlooked, but they've been consistently decent for a long time. • New York City FC: 7/10. A title win gives them a boost, but they still lack a true home. • New York Red Bulls: 6/10. Develop young talent, but rarely make a serious championship push. • Orlando City SC: 6/10. Some good moments, but inconsistent. Need to find more stability. • Philadelphia Union: 8/10. One of the best teams in the league right now, developing young talent and playing an exciting style. • Portland Timbers: 8/10. Intense atmosphere, loyal fans, and a history of success. They know how to win in the playoffs. • Real Salt Lake: 5/10. Can be dangerous in the playoffs, but inconsistent overall. • San Diego FC: 1/10. They're not even playing yet. • San Jose Earthquakes: 3/10. A mess. Consistently one of the worst teams in the league. • Seattle Sounders FC: 9/10. A model franchise with a great fanbase, consistent success, and a CONCACAF Champions League title. • Sporting Kansas City: 7/10. Has a great stadium and a history of success under Peter Vermes, but have been struggling a bit lately. • St. Louis City SC: 8/10. An instant hit with incredible fan support and a surprisingly good team. • Toronto FC: 5/10. Similar to Montreal, consistently shadowed and not very good. • Vancouver Whitecaps: 4/10. They're Canadian, but rarely make any noise in MLS.
