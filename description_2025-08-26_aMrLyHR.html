Alright, here's the brutally honest take on these albums, circa the 2000s, no holds barred: • Björk - Vespertine: Okay, this is that weird experimental shit that your artsy friend is obsessed with. It's cool that she's Icelandic and all, but honestly, it's background music for making a weird art project. Gets some respect for being different, but not something you blast in your Honda Civic. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust...: Bowie's the man, no question. "Ziggy Stardust" is a classic, even back then everyone knew it. Timeless cool, even if some of the outfits look a little sus now. • Godspeed You! Black Emperor - Lift Your Skinny Fists...: Super pretentious. It's like, 20 minutes of build-up for maybe 3 minutes of something interesting. Your college roommate probably listens to this while writing poetry about capitalism. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Oh God, who hurt you? This is the soundtrack to being terminally online and hating everything. It has its moments, but you probably wouldn't play it to your friends unless you want to drive them away. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: This is for when you're going through a breakup and need to wallow. Jeff's voice is incredible, but it's definitely a "feeling sad" record. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: Okay, this is important. Even if you don't love jazz rap, you gotta respect the message and the sheer talent. It's an album that makes you think, and it's got some killer beats. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: This is your dad's prog rock. A bit out there, but there's something undeniably cool about the musicianship. Probably impresses your older brother. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: A masterpiece. Everyone knows this album. Lauryn was the queen, and this album is timeless. No arguments here. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: Underground hip-hop at its finest. MF DOOM is a legend, and the beats are crazy. A bit weird for the mainstream, but if you know, you know. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Important, soulful, and still relevant. Everyone can appreciate this album, even if they're not super into soul music. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Intense. Jazz that's not for the faint of heart. It's challenging, but rewarding if you're willing to dive in. • Nas - Illmatic: Straight-up classic. This album is essential. If you don't like it, you probably don't like hip-hop. Simple as that. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: This is classic stoner rock. Perfect for late-night sessions with your friends. Timeless appeal. • Prince - Purple Rain: An absolute legend, Prince was one of the most talented artists of all time. This album has something for everyone and it's a certified banger • Radiohead - In Rainbows: The band that everyone loves, no matter what. It's artsy and cool, but also has some solid bangers that everyone likes, especially "Jigsaw Falling Into Place" • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: A bit long, but undeniably amazing. Stevie's a genius, and this album proves it. Soulful, funky, and full of amazing songs. • System of a Down - Toxicity: Raw, in your face, and totally insane. The political rants are wild, but the riffs are killer. A classic for anyone who wants to scream their head off. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: If you don't like the Beatles, you're either lying or deaf. This album is perfect, start to finish. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: Emo, but in a cool, lo-fi way. Great for those late nights where you just want to chill and contemplate life. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): Raw, gritty, and undeniably influential. This album changed hip-hop forever. "Protect Ya Neck" is still a banger.
