Alright, you want my take on these "albums" as if I were living in the 1930s? Here's what I think: • *Björk - Vespertine: Utterly bizarre. Sounds like noises emanating from an asylum, a collection of blips and burps. This isn't music; it's an insult to the great composers. 1/10. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: This sounds like some vaudeville act gone horribly wrong. The man sings as if he's got a frog in his throat, and the lyrics are nothing but gibberish. 2/10. • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: This is just dreadful, formless noise. A cacophony of instruments, building to some unholy crescendo. This music sounds like the end of the world is nigh. 0/10. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Depressing and morbid. The music is lethargic and seems intent on dragging the listener into the depths of despair. 1/10. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: Overwrought and histrionic. This young man has a fine voice, but he seems determined to wail and screech his way through every song. Less melodrama, more artistry, please! 4/10. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: Jarring and unpleasant. This "jazz" and "hip-hop" amalgamation is nothing but rhythmic shouting over a mishmash of clashing sounds. A waste of talent. 2/10. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: Bombastic and pretentious. This band seems determined to prove how clever they are, but the result is a confusing mess of noise. 3/10. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: A confused mix of styles. The girl has a nice voice, but the songs are meandering and unfocused. Needs more discipline. 4/10. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: Sloppy and incoherent. This "rap" music is just a bunch of mumbled words over a scratchy record. A complete waste of time. 1/10. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: A bit too modern for my tastes. The man has a good voice, but the songs are repetitive, and the lyrics are a bit too preachy. 5/10. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Chaotic and unsettling. This "jazz" music is all over the place, with no discernible melody or structure. A complete mess. 2/10. • Nas - Illmatic: Harsh and vulgar. This "rap" music is full of ugly language and violent imagery. It's a disgrace to the art form. 1/10. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Spacey and meandering. This music seems to drift off into the ether, with no clear direction or purpose. 3/10. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: Flashy and oversexed. The man has talent, but he seems more interested in showing off than in making good music. A bit too scandalous for my tastes. 4/10. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: Dreary and depressing. This music is like a wet blanket, dragging the listener down into the depths of despair. 2/10. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Pleasant but overlong. The man is talented, but the album is far too long and rambling. Needs some editing. 6/10. • System of a Down - Toxicity: Loud and obnoxious. This "rock" music is just a bunch of screaming and shouting. A complete assault on the ears. 1/10. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: Catchy but simplistic. These young men have a knack for writing catchy tunes, but their music lacks depth and sophistication. 5/10. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: Monotonous and dreary. This music seems to drone on and on, with no discernible melody or structure. Utterly boring. 1/10. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)***: Barbaric and offensive. This "rap" music is full of violence, vulgarity, and misogyny. A disgrace to society. 0/10. In conclusion, most of this modern "music" is a far cry from the classics of my day. Give me a good melody and some meaningful lyrics any day!
