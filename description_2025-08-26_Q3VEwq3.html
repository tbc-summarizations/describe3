Alright, you want a brutally honest take on these albums as if I were living in the 1970s? Buckle up. • Björk - Vespertine: (Inaudible sputtering) What in the unholy name of psychedelics is this Icelandic garbage? This is like the soundtrack to a nightmare I had after eating too much bad acid at Woodstock. Get this noise away from me. (Rating: Avoid at all costs, hippie nonsense) • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: Now THIS is more like it! Bowie's got the swagger, the glam, and the tunes! He's pushing boundaries, but in a way that's catchy and cool. He is the future of music! (Rating: A revolutionary masterpiece!) • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: Okay, I'm all for experimentation, but this is just self-indulgent noise. Endless instrumental rambling. Who has the time or patience for this? (Rating: Pure pretentious drivel. Turn it off.) • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Depressing music. This is definitely not a good idea, makes me want to kill myself. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: Too melodramatic. It's okay, but this is boring and sounds like screaming and crying, it feels like the artist is having some form of a breakdown. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: I think the artist is trying to have fun here but the beat is too weird. I would not listen to this. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: Now this is progressive rock done right! Moody, complex, and atmospheric. It takes you on a journey. Even if you are driving to work. (Rating: A stone-cold classic! Listen under the stars.) • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: Honestly, this is a bit all over the place. It's like she's trying to do too much at once. I think it can make her and her record label some money. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: What is this trash? I have no idea what's going on here. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Marvin's hitting some heavy truths here, and it's beautiful. A soul album that speaks to the times. It's a shame so many are not hearing it. (Rating: Essential listening. Gets your head right.) • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Mingus, he knows it and he is able to turn that into music. That man knows how to play, and this is jazz that demands to be listened to (Rating: Mind-blowing.) • Nas - Illmatic: I cannot listen to this. This is noise to me. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Floyd is starting to get a little too spacey, but this album has some solid moments. Good for a late night when you are in a dark room. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: He is not a good person. I hate his music and who he is. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: Radiohead? It is okay. Not horrible. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Stevie can do no wrong. This is a musical gift. A joyous, soulful masterpiece. (Rating: Pure sunshine in vinyl form! Get this in your collection ASAP.) • System of a Down - Toxicity: A band to make you break your speakers. It feels like angry teenagers trying to change the world. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: This is a must have, one of the best to ever do it. I am not going to get into it. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: This is the worst thing I have ever heard. I would never listen to this. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): Wu-Tang? More like Wu-Tang-CRAP! I feel like these guys are annoying and will get no where in life. There you have it. Brutally honest opinions from a jaded 1970s music fan. Some of these would've been hits, others would've bombed hard!
