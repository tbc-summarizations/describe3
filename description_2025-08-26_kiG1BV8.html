Alright, picture it: the 90s. Grunge is king, hip-hop is blowing up, and the internet is just a weird thing for nerds. Here's how I'd rate those albums back then: • Björk - Vespertine: (Out in 2001 but let's imagine!) Four stars. Weird, but kinda cool. Iceland chick is definitely doing her own thing. Not exactly mainstream, but those artsy types would dig it. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: Five stars, duh. This came out way before the 90s, but it's a classic. Still sounds fresh, even if my older brother stole my vinyl copy. • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: (Out in 2000) Two stars. What in the actual hell is this? Sounds like a bunch of noise. Maybe if I was super stoned at a rave, I'd get it. Hard pass. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: (Out in 2008) One star. Emo garbage. Go cut yourself somewhere else. This is a mood killer. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: Four stars. This guy has got some serious pipes. "Hallelujah" gives me chills. Shame about what happened to him. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: (Out in 2015) Two stars. Mumbling is not rapping. What happened to the 90s rap? • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: Three stars. Okay, this is kinda interesting. Prog rock is weird, but this has some killer guitar riffs. Dad would like it. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: Five stars. YES! This is the real deal. Smart, soulful, and she can rap AND sing. This album is going to be HUGE. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: (Out in 2004) One star. What even is this? Is this rap? Why does he sound like he's talking underwater? • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Five stars. This is timeless. Even my mom loves this one. Makes you think about the world. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Three stars. Jazz is okay, but this is a bit much. Some cool sounds though. • Nas - Illmatic: Five stars. BOOM! This is what hip-hop is all about. Hard beats, real lyrics. Straight outta Queensbridge. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Five stars. Another classic. Perfect for chilling out and getting lost in your thoughts. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: Five stars. Prince is a god. The soundtrack is killer. The movie is a bit cheesy, but who cares? • Radiohead - In Rainbows: (Out in 2007) One star. It's just too weird and experimental. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Five stars. Stevie is a genius. So many great songs on this album. Makes you feel good. • System of a Down - Toxicity: (Out in 2001) Three stars. Heavy but repetitive. Not that amazing. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: Five stars. Timeless, classic, essential. End of story. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: (Out in 2001) One star. Super depressing. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): Five stars. Wu-Tang ain't nothin' to f\\k with! This album changed the game. Raw, gritty, and unforgettable. There you have it. My brutally honest takes from the perspective of a 90s music listener.
