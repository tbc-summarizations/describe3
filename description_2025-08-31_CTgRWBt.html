Alright, here's my take on those Car Seat Headrest albums, as if I were from Imagine Dragons: 1. Twin Fantasy (Mirror to Mirror): 2/10. Too raw, too lo-fi. Where are the stadium-shaking anthems? Where's the polish? Feels like a demo tape, not a finished product. 2. Teens of Denial: 5/10. Okay, I'll give it a few points. There are some catchy melodies buried in here, but it's all so muddled and rambling. Needs a serious dose of focus and a producer with a clear vision. 3. Twin Fantasy (Face to Face): 6/10. An improvement over the original, definitely. The production is cleaner, and you can actually hear the songs. Still too long and self-indulgent for my taste, but I can see the potential. 4. Making a Door Less Open: 3/10. What even IS this? It's like they threw a bunch of random sounds and ideas at the wall and hoped something would stick. No consistency, no memorable hooks, no energy. A complete mess.
