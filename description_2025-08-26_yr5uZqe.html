Alright, here's the brutally honest truth about these albums, rated like I'm back in the 2000s, ready to drop some serious opinions: • Björk - Vespertine: 7/10. Still weird, still Björk. Some of those quieter moments are kinda boring though. Definitely not as immediate as "Homogenic." • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: 10/10. Total classic. Even in the 2000s, you gotta respect the glam rock god. Timeless. • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: 6/10. Way too long. Like, I get the whole "epic" thing, but this is just self-indulgent noise for hipsters. Snoozefest. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: 8/10. Emo, depressing, but kinda cool. A little too lo-fi for its own good. It is a bit overrated, though. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: 9/10. What a voice! Still breaks your heart. Only docking a point because it could have been better. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: 9/10. Damn. This album shows some real skill. The lyricism is excellent, but some moments are kinda boring. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: 8/10. Prog rock is pretty cool, and this one is the best, but it just feels old and boring. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: 10/10. Amazing. This album has everything to offer, and it is incredible. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: 10/10. DOOM is a legend. Weird, awesome beats, and lyrics that are just out there. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: 10/10. Can't argue with a masterpiece. So relevant even now. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: 7/10. Jazz is cool, but this is a bit much for me. A little too chaotic. • Nas - Illmatic: 10/10. The greatest hip-hop album ever made. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: 9/10. Classic, but "Dark Side" is better. Still a great album though. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: 10/10. Iconic. Prince was a genius. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: 10/10. Ahead of its time. Just a great album. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: 9/10. Incredible talent. Long, but mostly worth it. • System of a Down - Toxicity: 10/10. This album has everything! Amazing, brutal, and hilarious! The greatest album ever made. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: 10/10. The Beatles are the Beatles. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: 6/10. The singer sounds pretty bad, the album is nothing special. I guess it's good but it's whatever. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): 10/10. Wu-Tang Clan forever. This album is the reason Wu-Tang is famous. Incredible.
