Alright, here's my brutally honest take on those albums, judging them with 2020s ears and sensibilities: • Björk - Vespertine: Still sounds fresh and innovative. Björk was ahead of her time, and this album's blend of electronic textures and intimate vocals holds up incredibly well. Rating: 9/10. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust...: Overrated. It's historically important, but honestly, the songwriting is inconsistent and the glam rock sound is dated. Some bangers, but not the masterpiece people claim. Rating: 6/10. • Godspeed You! Black Emperor - Lift Your Skinny Fists...: A pretentious slog. Way too long, repetitive, and devoid of anything resembling hooks. I get the "atmosphere," but give me a break. Rating: 4/10. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Edgy internet music for sad boys. It has its moments, but it's way too melodramatic and the lo-fi production is more of a crutch than an artistic choice. Rating: 5/10. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: Seriously overrated. It's technically impressive, but Buckley's vocals are often theatrical to the point of being annoying. "Hallelujah" is a beautiful song that has been ruined by overplay. Rating: 5/10. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: A stone-cold classic. This album is still incredibly relevant and powerful. The production is innovative, the lyrics are dense and insightful, and Kendrick's delivery is flawless. Rating: 10/10. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: Still bizarre and unsettling. I can see the influence, but it doesn't really do it for me. Rating: 6/10 • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: I'll admit this is a classic album. The lyrical content and delivery still resonates with me. There's filler but overall still great. Rating: 8/10 • Madvillain - Madvillainy: Criminally overrated. It's lo-fi to a fault. The production is muddy and the rapping is mumbled. I don't get the hype. Rating: 4/10. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Timeless and essential. The social commentary is still relevant, and the music is beautiful and soulful. Rating: 10/10. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Too chaotic and avant-garde for my taste. I respect the ambition, but it's not something I'd listen to for pleasure. Rating: 5/10. • Nas - Illmatic: Still a masterpiece of storytelling and lyricism. The production is perfect, and Nas's delivery is effortless. Rating: 10/10. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Nostalgic and well-produced, but ultimately boring. "Shine On You Crazy Diamond" is a masterpiece, but the rest of the album is forgettable. Rating: 6/10. • Prince - Purple Rain: A pop masterpiece. The songs are catchy, the performances are electrifying, and Prince is at the peak of his powers. Rating: 9/10. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: Still one of the best albums of the 2000s. The production is lush and atmospheric, and the songwriting is top-notch. Rating: 9/10. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Overlong and self-indulgent. There are some incredible songs on here, but there's also a lot of filler. Needs editing. Rating: 7/10. • System of a Down - Toxicity: Still holds up. The aggression is raw, the lyrics are nonsensical but fun, and the musicianship is impressive. Rating: 8/10. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: A fitting end to the Beatles' career. The songwriting is strong, the production is inventive, and the performances are polished. A bit overrated though. Rating: 8/10 • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: More pretentious indie crap. Okay, it's not bad, but it's nothing special. The DIY aesthetic is more annoying than charming. Rating: 5/10. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter the Wu-Tang: Changed the game, but honestly, it sounds kind of dated now. The production is gritty, the rapping is raw, and the overall vibe is menacing. Rating: 7/10.
