Alright, here's my brutally honest rating of Italy's 2006 World Cup winning starting XI: Individual Player Ratings: • Gianluigi Buffon: 9/10. A world-class goalkeeper at his peak. His shot-stopping was phenomenal and he was a commanding presence in the box. A true legend. • Gianluca Zambrotta: 7/10. Solid and dependable, but not spectacular. He did his job well defensively and offered some attacking threat, but not a standout performer. • Fabio Cannavaro: 10/10. An absolute rock at the back. His performances in that tournament were arguably the best of his career. Impeccable positioning, tackling, and leadership. • Marco Materazzi: 6/10. Controversial and prone to errors, but he stepped up when needed. His goal in the final was crucial, but overall, his performances were inconsistent. • Fabio Grosso: 7/10. Scored the winning penalty in the final and was solid defensively. However, he wasn't always the most convincing defensively. • Mauro Camoranesi: 6/10. Work rate was great, but lacked the quality of some of his teammates. Not the most creative player, but did his job. • Gennaro Gattuso: 8/10. The engine of the team. His relentless tackling and energy were vital in protecting the defense. A true warrior. • Andrea Pirlo: 9/10. The brains of the team. His vision, passing range, and set-piece ability were exceptional. Dictated the tempo of the game and was always a threat. • Simone Perrotta: 5/10. The weak link in the midfield. Did a job, but lacks flair and creativity. • Francesco Totti: 7/10. Scored some important goals, but not at his best. His creativity and passing were still good, but he wasn't as explosive as in his prime. • Luca Toni: 6/10. Led the line well and scored a couple of goals, but wasn't prolific. His hold-up play and aerial ability were useful, but not a world-beater. Team Rating: 8/10. A well-organized and disciplined team with a strong defense and midfield. They lacked some flair and firepower up front, but their tactical nous and teamwork were exceptional. They were a worthy winner of the World Cup.
