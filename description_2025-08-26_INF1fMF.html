Alright, let's dive into this list like it's 1985 and I'm spinning these on my turntable, judging them with the discerning ears of a seasoned music fan from the era of big hair and even bigger sounds: • Björk - Vespertine: (Release way ahead of it's time) This sounds like some weird art-school project gone wrong. All those digital beeps and whispers? Give me a good synthesizer any day. Björk needs to find a real band. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: Now this is more like it! Bowie knows how to put on a show. Ziggy Stardust is pure glam rock genius. One of his best, even compared to his earlier work. • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: (Release way ahead of it's time) What in the name of new wave is this garbage? It's like a bunch of noise with no beat. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: (Release way ahead of it's time) Depressing, slow and boring. I'm not sure what type of music this is even attempting to be. I feel like my brain is melting just listening to it. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: (Release way ahead of it's time) This guy can sing, that's for sure. But it's a bit too sentimental for my taste. Could be a one-hit wonder. Let's see if he can keep it up. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: (Release way ahead of it's time) Hip-hop mixed with jazz? Get out of here. It will never work. There is no way this guy is going to make a hit from this. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: I have no idea what this is, but this is just a cacophony of noise to me. If this got any attention, then I would avoid it like the plague. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: (Release way ahead of it's time) I like some of the soul and R&B in this, but the rapping is kind of amateur. It will not last long. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: (Release way ahead of it's time) Another rapper with a gimmick. It's unique, but it's not going to get him anywhere. People don't want different they want generic. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Marvin is at it again! His voice is still angelic and his songs still hit deep. What's Going On is an instant classic and will be talked about for years. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: This is nothing but noise. I will probably not be playing it again. • Nas - Illmatic: (Release way ahead of it's time) This guy can rap, alright. But he's just another gangster wannabe, talking about the same old stuff. It's not going to be a big deal. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Floyd did it again! Their music just gets better and better. From the guitar riffs to the catchy vocals it just works. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: Now here's an artist who knows how to mix funk, rock, and a whole lot of attitude! I can play this when I am sad, happy or angry. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: (Release way ahead of it's time) More depressing garbage. What is wrong with people these days? Make me smile at least. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Stevie can do no wrong. The funk and pop sound is infectious and will make you dance for days. • System of a Down - Toxicity: (Release way ahead of it's time) A bunch of angry garbage. What is with these guys just yelling into microphones. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: Everyone knows the Beatles. Their music just is an instant hit that has some good rock-and-roll sounds. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: (Release way ahead of it's time) Nothing but some indie nonsense. It will never get played anywhere but a college dorm. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): (Release way ahead of it's time) The rapping is cool and all, but this is another flash in the pan. It's just trying to hard to be edgy.
