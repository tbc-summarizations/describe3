Alright, here's my take on these Car Seat Headrest albums, like I'm Imagine Dragons. Get ready for some real talk: 1. Sunburned Shirts: 3/10. A bit too lo-fi for our stadium sound. Needs more epic build-ups. 2. My Back Is Killing Me Baby: 4/10. I respect the raw emotion, but it's just too much of a downer. Where's the anthemic energy? 3. Twin Fantasy (Mirror to Mirror): 5/10. It's got a certain charm, but the production is rough. Needs a serious glow-up in the studio. 4. Monomania: 6/10. I can appreciate the darker themes, but it lacks a certain polish. Needs a bigger sound. 5. Starving While Living: 3/10. I'm not loving the production or energy. It's just not for me, sorry. 6. Nervous Young Man: 5/10. It's got a story, but the execution falls short. Where's the soaring vocals and dramatic instrumentation? 7. How To Leave Town: 4/10. Too experimental and disjointed for our taste. Needs more focus and structure. 8. Teens of Style: 7/10. Now we're talking! Catchy hooks and a tighter sound. Still needs some work, but it's on the right track. 9. Teens of Denial: 8/10. Solid songwriting and more polished production. I can actually see myself enjoying this one. 10. Twin Fantasy (Face to Face): 6/10. I'm not a huge fan, not really anything I would play for enjoyment 11. Making a Door Less Open: 5/10. Eh, a bit too weird for us. Needs more accessible melodies and sing-along choruses. 12. MADLO: Influences: 4/10. Too derivative and uninspired. Needs more originality and experimentation. 13. The Scholars: 2/10. Absolutely the worst piece of crap I have ever heard. There you have it. Brutally honest, just like you asked.
