Alright, here's my totally honest, Nickelback-approved take on these Fishmans albums: • Chappie, Don't Cry: 3/10 - Sounds like the kind of thing you'd hear at a dentist's office. Elevator music with a weird accent. • King Master George: 4/10 - A little more energy, but still feels like I'm stuck in a cheesy 80s arcade game. • Neo Yankees' Holiday: 5/10 - Okay, now we're getting somewhere. Catchy enough to not make me wanna smash my guitar, but still needs more distortion and less... whatever that is. • ORANGE: 6/10 - Has its moments, but it's all over the place. Feels like they're trying too hard to be artsy. • 空中キャンプ \[Kūchū Camp]: 5/10 - More of the same. Drifting along, not really going anywhere. Someone wake me up when the riffs kick in. • LONG SEASON: 8/10 - Now this I can get behind. It's got a pulse, a groove, and some actual emotion. Still a little weird, but in a good way. Reminds me of our slower songs that the ladies love. • 宇宙 日本 世田谷 \[Uchu Nippon Setagaya]: 7/10 - Similar to Orange, but a bit more focused. Still got that weirdness, but it's working for them here. • 98.12.28 男達の別れ \[98.12.28 Otokotachi no wakare]: 9/10 - Epic. This is what I'm talking about! Raw energy, emotion, and enough guitar to make me wanna crack open a beer. If they just wrote a few more songs like this, they might actually be rock stars.
