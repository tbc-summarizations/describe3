Alright mate, here's my brutally honest take on these Madlib albums, as if I were laying down some tracks in the studio: • Shades Of Blue: Madlib Invades Blue Note: 8/10 - A solid re-imagining of classics. Has some real depth, but maybe a bit too heady for the dancefloor. • Champion Sound (with J Dilla): 9/10 - This is where it's at! Raw, innovative, and full of energy. You can hear the genius in both of them. • Madvillainy (with MF DOOM): 10/10 - An absolute masterpiece. DOOM's lyrical genius over Madlib's twisted beats is just perfection. • Vol. 1: Movie Scenes: 6/10 - Interesting concept, but lacks some real punch. Good for chilling, not for raging. • Vol. 2: Movie Scenes, the Sequel: 5/10 - More of the same, and doesn't improve on the original. • Liberation (with Talib Kweli): 7/10 - A respectable collab, good flows and conscious themes. But I didn't like it all that much. • Vol. 3: Beat Konducta in India: 4/10 - Some nice samples, but gets a bit repetitive and loses my interest. • Vol. 4: Beat Konducta in India: 4/10 - Same as Vol 3. • Vol. 5: Dil Cosby Suite: 5/10 - Meh. • WLIB AM: King of the Wigflip: 4/10 - Not a fan. • Vol. 6: Dil Withers Suite: 5/10 - Meh again. • Madlib Medicine Show: No. 3 - Beat Konducta in Africa: 4/10 - A bit too disjointed for my taste, lacks a clear direction. • Rock Konducta Part 1: 6/10 - Finally something a bit different! Some interesting takes on rock, but could be tighter. • Blackmarket Seminar: 7/10 - Getting a bit more interesting again. Some solid grooves here. • Piñata (with Freddie Gibbs): 9/10 - Gibbs brings the fire, and Madlib provides the perfect backdrop. Gritty and real. • Rock Konducta Pt. 2: 6/10 - Same as part 1. • Bad Neighbor (with MED and Blu): 7/10 - A decent effort, but not as memorable as some of his other work. • Bandana (with Freddie Gibbs): 9/10 - They went hard with this one. • Sound Ancestors: 7/10 - Interesting and ambitious, a solid effort from Madlib. • In the Beginning, Vol. 1: 6/10 - Some decent stuff, nothing that will wow you. • In the Beginning, Vol. 2: 5/10 - Same as Vol 1. • In The Beginning Vol. 3: 5/10 - And same as Vol 2. • Liberation 2 (with Talib Kweli): 8/10 - Now this is what I'm talking about! • Champagne for Breakfast: 6/10 - A bit of a misstep if you ask me. • I'm Alive: 5/10 - Boring. • YHWH Is Love: 4/10 - What is even this lol? • Seeds (Instrumentals): 6/10 - Not a bad effort but not the greatest.
