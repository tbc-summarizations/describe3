Alright, here's the brutally honest assessment of those albums as if I'm judging them from the perspective of someone living in the 2010s: • Björk - Vespertine: Still weird and kinda pretentious. Some people will claim it's genius, but most just find it inaccessible and overly precious. Overrated. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: Okay, Bowie's legacy is undeniable, but honestly, glam rock sounds kinda cheesy now. This album is still influential, but feels dated. • Godspeed You Black Emperor! - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: Seriously, who has time for this? It's instrumental, long, and depressing. Probably only listened to by hipsters who wear exclusively black clothing. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: Emo kids will eat this up. It's depressing, poorly produced, and tries way too hard to be edgy. It's trying way too hard to get internet clout. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: Over-the-top vocals and melodramatic lyrics. Kind of a cliché "sensitive guy" album. It's alright but not groundbreaking. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: Finally, something actually good! Kendrick is a genius. The album is still culturally relevant and still hits as hard as it did when it came out. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: Prog rock? Get out of here. No one cares about 70s art-school experiments anymore. It does sound good, but it's extremely pretentious and inaccessible. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: Timeless. Lauryn Hill is a queen and this album is a masterpiece. Still relevant and still fire. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: Still a classic. Still weird, and DOOM's still as enigmatic as ever. It's still an underground classic, although it might be a bit too niche for mainstream appeal. • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: Absolutely essential. A classic for a reason. Still relevant with it's political and social themes. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: Jazz is boring. Pass. • Nas - Illmatic: Nas is a legend. This is still a landmark hip-hop album and the production is amazing. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: Overplayed. It's alright, but people need to stop acting like it's the greatest album ever made. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: Prince is a god. This album is iconic, and the title track is legendary. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: Still influential, still depressing, still good. It's still considered one of Radiohead's best albums, but people are kind of tired of hearing "Nude". • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: Classic, but a bit long. Some filler, but the hits are undeniable. • System of a Down - Toxicity: Still a banger. It's still a great album, even though some of the lyrics are kinda nonsensical. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: Beatles are overrated. It's a good album, but people need to chill out. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: Indieheads are so predictable. Overhyped and sounds like it was recorded in a trashcan. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter The Wu-Tang (36 Chambers): Wu-Tang is for the children. This album is essential hip-hop.
