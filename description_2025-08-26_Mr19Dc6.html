Alright, here's the brutally honest, 2010s-tinted take on these albums: • *Björk - Vespertine**: 7/10. It's Björk, so it's weird and intriguing, but it's also kinda...static? It doesn't hit as hard as Homogenic, and a lot of the glitchy stuff feels a bit dated now that everyone's doing hyperpop. • David Bowie - The Rise and Fall of Ziggy Stardust and the Spiders from Mars: 9/10. Timeless. Still feels like the future even today. Everyone from Gaga to Arcade Fire owes something to this record. It's just such a cool and unique concept. • Godspeed You! Black Emperor - Lift Your Skinny Fists Like Antennas to Heaven: 6/10. Okay, it's epic, I get it. But let's be real, how often are you REALLY in the mood for this? It's like the music equivalent of reading a Russian novel - impressive, but a chore. • Have a Nice Life - Deathconsciousness: 8/10. Bleak and atmospheric, this album is the ultimate emo mood for late-night feels. Its got a strong cult following. • Jeff Buckley - Grace: 9/10. Buckley's voice is just unreal. Every singer wishes they can hit those notes. Plus, "Hallelujah" is forever iconic, even if it's been covered to death. • Kendrick Lamar - To Pimp a Butterfly: 10/10. This album is mandatory listening. It's complex, it's important, and it slaps. You don't just listen to it, you experience it. • King Crimson - In the Court of the Crimson King: 5/10. I appreciate the ambition, but this is the kind of prog rock that gets made fun of. Mellotrons and fantasy lyrics? C'mon, guys. • Lauryn Hill - The Miseducation of Lauryn Hill: 10/10. Still relevant, still powerful. She's spitting straight fire about love, society, and everything in between. She set a new standard. • Madvillain - Madvillainy: 9/10. MF DOOM is a lyrical genius and Madlib's beats are perfect. It's weird, it's funky, it's endlessly replayable. It has some really hardcore fans • Marvin Gaye - What's Going On: 10/10. Timeless. This album is as relevant now as it was back then. Marvin lays down some seriously uncomfortable truths about the state of the world, and it still hits hard. • Mingus - The Black Saint and the Sinner Lady: 7/10. Avant-garde jazz that can be challenging to get into. It's more of an academic exercise than something you'd casually throw on. • Nas - Illmatic: 10/10. Lyrically dense, and the beats are gritty and raw. It's a snapshot of a specific time and place, and it's perfect. • Pink Floyd - Wish You Were Here: 9/10. Still sounds amazing with headphones. Its a great record about loss. • Prince and the Revolution - Purple Rain: 10/10. Just all-around amazing. • Radiohead - In Rainbows: 9/10. Radiohead at their most accessible and emotional. The "pay-what-you-want" release was also a game-changer. • Stevie Wonder - Songs in the Key of Life: 10/10. A masterclass in songwriting and musicianship. It's long, but every song is amazing. Stevie's on another level. • System of a Down - Toxicity: 8/10. It's chaotic, it's aggressive, and it's impossible to ignore. You can't deny the sheer energy and originality of this band. • The Beatles - Abbey Road: 9/10. It's the Beatles, so of course it's great. It's polished, it's experimental, and it's a perfect send-off to the greatest band of all time. • The Microphones - The Glow, Pt. 2: 8/10. Lo-fi and intimate, this album creates a whole world of its own. It's like listening to someone's diary. • Wu-Tang Clan - Enter the Wu-Tang (36 Chambers)***: 10/10. The beats are hard, the rhymes are insane, and the whole vibe is just menacing. It's a game-changer.
